Align

Smart glasses concept that helps reduce and prevent Tech Neck through real-time feedback.

Problem

Students and professionals spend long hours on screens, often developing poor posture habits such as forward head tilt without noticing until discomfort occurs.

Existing solutions like reminder apps are often ignored or disruptive, making them ineffective for sustained behaviour change.

User Research

Secondary research (Australian Chiropractors Association, 2025):

  • 64% of Australians experience neck pain linked to device use
  • 7/10 report reduced daily comfort and movement
  • Only 36% are aware their pain is screen-related
  • Regular breaks reduce neck pain by up to 33%

Key Insights

  • Users only notice posture issues after pain develops
  • Reminder-based systems are ignored
  • Prevention is more effective than correction
  • Poor ergonomics are common in study/work environments

Design Impact

  • Real-time posture detection over delayed feedback
  • Subtle haptic correction instead of alerts
  • Minimal interruption design for focus-heavy tasks

Solution

Align provides real-time posture detection through smart glasses and delivers subtle haptic feedback to encourage immediate correction.

The focus is on behavioural change during screen use, not after it.

Key Features

  • Real-time posture detection using sensors
  • Subtle haptic feedback for correction cues
  • Adaptive sensitivity based on user behaviour
  • Posture insights and usage tracking
  • Passive operation with no interaction required
  • Lightweight wearable design for daily use

Design Decisions

  • Haptic feedback chosen over alerts to avoid distraction
  • Glasses form factor for natural daily use
  • Passive system to reduce user effort and friction
  • Focused on forward head posture only
  • Privacy-first design avoiding camera tracking

My Role

  • Defined problem through research and observation
  • Conducted user research and synthesized insights
  • Developed concept and interaction model
  • Designed UX approach focused on minimal distraction
  • Structured full case study for portfolio presentation

Reflection

What I learned:

  • How important real-time feedback is when designing for behaviour change, rather than relying on reminders or alerts.
  • That even simple physical habits like posture are difficult to change without subtle and consistent reinforcement.
  • How secondary research can strongly shape and justify design decisions in the absence of primary testing.
  • The importance of keeping wearable technology minimal and non-intrusive to support long-term user adoption.

Skills developed:

  • Thinking through how a wearable device would realistically fit into daily study and work routines.
  • Breaking down posture correction into simple, real-time interactions that don’t interrupt focus.
  • Using secondary research and data to support design decisions instead of assumptions.
  • Designing with real-world limitations in mind, such as user distraction and device fatigue.

Next Steps

Future development would involve doing empathy fieldwork, prototyping the system and testing its effectiveness in real-world scenarios, as well as exploring additional posture correction cues and long-term user engagement strategies. Once I have more free time I would love to explore this project further and turn it into a real prototype.
